Traveller Trade and Ship Management Guide

A comprehensive step-by-step guide to conducting trade and managing ships in Traveller.

High-Level Overview

  1. Prepare for Trade: Ensure the ship has sufficient space, funds, and crew.
  2. Select a Cargo Type or Service: Choose between speculative trade, freight, mail, or passengers.
  3. Find Opportunities: Use the rules to locate suppliers, passengers, or mail contracts.
  4. Negotiate Terms: Roll skill checks to determine purchase or sale prices or secure contracts.
  5. Travel to the Destination: Account for fuel, travel time, and potential hazards en route.
  6. Deliver and Collect Payment: Deliver goods, mail, or passengers and calculate final profits.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Freight Transport

  1. Determine Freight Availability: Roll 2D on the Freight Traffic table (p. 239) and apply modifiers like starport type and population size.
  2. Freight Types:
    • Incidental: 1D tons (DM+2 availability).
    • Minor: 1D x 5 tons.
    • Major: 1D x 10 tons (DM-4 availability).
  3. Payment: Freight pays based on distance. Refer to the Passage and Freight Table (p. 239).

Step 2: Mail Delivery

  1. Check for Mail Contracts: Roll 2D on the Mail Table (p. 240). Each container occupies 5 tons and pays Cr25,000 per container.
  2. Modifiers: Include Freight Traffic DM and ship armaments.

Step 3: Passengers

  1. Find Passengers: Roll 2D on the Passenger Traffic Table for each type (High, Middle, Basic, Low). Modifiers depend on starport type and population.
  2. Passenger Types:
    • High Passage: Cr9,000/parsec.
    • Middle Passage: Cr6,500/parsec.
    • Basic Passage: Cr2,000/parsec.
    • Low Passage: Cr700/parsec.

Step 4: Speculative Trade

  1. Find a Supplier: Use skill checks (e.g., Broker, Streetwise) to locate goods (p. 241).
  2. Determine Goods and Prices: Roll 3D and consult the Modified Price Table (p. 243).
  3. Sell Goods: Travel to a new world, locate buyers, and repeat the price calculation process.

Step 5: Refueling and Maintenance

  1. Docking Fees: Landing fees vary by starport type (p. 154).
  2. Fuel Costs: Refined fuel costs Cr500/ton; unrefined fuel costs Cr100/ton.

Step 6: Combine Activities

Optimize profitability by combining passengers, freight, mail, and speculative trade during a single journey. Efficient route planning and timing are crucial.
