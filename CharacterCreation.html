Step-by-Step Guide to Traveller Creation with Page References

Below is a structured, step-by-step guide to creating a character in the Traveller RPG, referencing the 2022 Core Rulebook (pages 8–57). This process includes rules for rolling characteristics, choosing careers, and developing skills and history.

High-Level Overview

  1. Roll Characteristics (pg. 8-9): Generate the six core attributes of the character.
  2. Choose Background Skills (pg. 9): Select skills based on your character’s Education modifier (EDU DM).
  3. Choose a Career Path (pg. 11): Enter a career and follow its rules for development.
  4. Develop Skills and Events (pg. 12-50): Advance through career terms, gaining skills, benefits, and encountering life events.
  5. Resolve Aging Effects (pg. 49): Apply effects if the character ages past a certain threshold.
  6. Finalize Character (pg. 50-57): Choose personal goals, define relationships, and select a skill package.

Detailed Instructions

Step 1: Roll Characteristics (pg. 8-9)

  • Roll 2D6 six times and assign the scores to the following attributes:
    • Strength (STR): Physical strength and forcefulness.
    • Dexterity (DEX): Coordination and reflexes.
    • Endurance (END): Stamina and resilience.
    • Intellect (INT): Quickness of mind and intelligence.
    • Education (EDU): Formal learning and knowledge.
    • Social Standing (SOC): Position in society.
  • Dice Modifiers (DM): Calculate the DM for each attribute using the table:
    • 0-2 = -2
    • 3-5 = -1
    • 6-8 = +0
    • 9-11 = +1
    • 12-14 = +2
    • 15+ = +3
  • Alternate Rules: Assign scores in any order or roll attributes sequentially.

Step 2: Background Skills (pg. 9)

  • Determine Number of Skills: Calculate EDU DM + 3 to determine how many background skills your character receives.
  • Choose Skills: Select from the following:
    • Admin, Animals, Art, Athletics, Carouse, Drive, Electronics, Flyer, Language, Mechanic, Medic, Profession, Science, Seafarer, Streetwise, Survival, Vacc Suit.
  • These skills represent the character's adolescence and basic societal functionality.

Step 3: Pre-Career Education (Optional) (pg. 13)

  • Before starting a career, you may attend University or a Military Academy.
  • Entry Roll: Roll 2D6. A failed roll forces the character to start a career instead.
  • Success allows access to specific skills or bonuses.
  • Duration: Each education term lasts four years.

Step 4: Career Selection (pg. 11-12)

  • Choose a career that reflects the character’s path before adventuring. Careers include Agent, Merchant, Drifter, etc.
  • Entry Roll: Each career has specific requirements. Failing this roll may force a draft or alternate career.
  • Assignments: Choose a sub-specialization within the career.

Step 5: Career Development (pg. 12-50)

  • Each career term represents four years. For each term:
    • Survival Roll: Determine if the character continues in their career.
    • Events: Roll 2D6 on the career’s Event Table for significant events.
    • Skills: Gain career-related skills and specializations.
    • Advancement Roll: Check if the character advances in rank.
  • Mishaps: Failing a Survival Roll forces the character out of the career and results in a Mishap.
  • Connections Rule (pg. 19): Collaborate with other players to establish character ties and backstory.

Step 6: Benefits and Aging (pg. 49)

  • Benefits: Upon leaving a career, roll for cash and equipment benefits based on career rank.
  • Aging: If the character is 34 or older, roll for aging effects. Attributes may degrade over time.

Step 7: Finalizing the Character (pg. 50-57)

  • Choose Goals: Define the character’s motivations and objectives.
  • Relationships: Establish Allies, Rivals, and Enemies.
  • Skill Packages: Select a group skill package to round out the party’s capabilities. Examples include Starship, Explorer, and Criminal Packages (pg. 50).

Additional Notes

  • Augments: Characters may purchase biological or cybernetic enhancements later in the game (pg. 106).
  • Equipment: Refer to page 97 for gear and weapons.
